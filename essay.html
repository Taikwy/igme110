  • CLOCKWORK MANSION

Video games nowadays can be based in all sorts of settings, although few can top the fascinating whalepunk aesthetic of the Dishonored franchise. The coasts of the Kingdom of Dunwall and the Island of Serkonos aren’t the only thing unique about Dishonored; from the art style, to the music, down to the very core design of the game Dishonored tries to break the mold. The franchise is no stranger to extremely memorable levels, yet few are as easily recognizable as the Clockwork Mansion. In terms of both narrative and gameplay significance, the Clockwork Mansion stands out as one of the most iconic levels in Dishonored 2. As a product of the success of the reveal trailer for Dishonored 2, I was completely hooked. The sprawling clockwork mechanisms and fluid movement of the trailer captivated me, and I was more than eager to see how well Arkane Studios had transferred their CG trailer into the game. At first the level seemed to be just as beautiful and flawless as the trailer promised. After just a bit of gameplay however, my hopes had been slashed and I realized the Clockwork Mansion had failed to meet any of my expectations at all. What should have been a shining jewel amidst the other incredible levels in Dishonored 2, the Clockwork Mansion succeeds only at being one of the most disappointing levels I’ve ever played in a video game. For a mansion made of clockwork with such potential for level design and gameplay, the Clockwork Wang 2 Mansion stands as the worst level in Dishonored 2 and a prime example of biting off far more than you can chew.
